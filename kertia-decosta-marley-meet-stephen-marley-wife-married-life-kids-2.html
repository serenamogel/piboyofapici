Stephen Marley is a Grammy Award-winning Jamaican-American musician. Many visitors want to know more about Stephen Marley’s wife. Stephen Marley is a Jamaican-American singer and composer born Stephen Robert Nesta Marley.

He is the son of Bob Marley and a founding member of Ziggy Marley and The Melody Makers. Marley decided to go solo after years of working as a background producer and supporting older brother Ziggy as a melody developer. Mind Control, his debut album, was released in 2007.

He received a Grammy nomination for Best Reggae Album for his first three solo albums. He has won eight Grammy Awards. Stephen Marley’s wife is Kertia DeCosta Marley. Their wedding date and location were previously undisclosed. While no further details about Kertia DeCosta Marley have been disclosed, we do know that the couple split a few years ago. They maintain a relationship outside of the marriage.

Stephen Marley’s Family and Children

Marley was born on April 20, 1972, in Kingston, Jamaica, to parents Bob Marley and Rita Marley. He was raised in Kingston, Jamaica after being born in Wilmington, Delaware. Stephen has 11 siblings in total. Four came from his father, Bob, and mother, Rita; two came from his mother, Rita’s former relationship, and the other from Bob’s earlier partnerships.

In a private ceremony, Stephen married Kertia DeCosta Marley. Stephen, like his father, has eleven children: Joseph Mersa “Jo Mersa” Marley, Binghi Marley, Nyah Marley, Summer Marley, Mystic Marley, Sasha Marley, Jeremiah Marley, Zipporah Marley, Yohan Marley, Lailah Marley, and Shacia Marley.

Stephen’s children are also the heirs to their father and grandfather’s Reggae music legacy. His oldest son, Joseph Marley, published his debut EP, Comfortable, while his daughter, Mystic Marley, released her first single and music video, Beatdown.

Stephen Marley Ethnicity

Stephen is of Jamaican-American descent and has a full Jamaican heritage. His father was a Rastafari (a religion founded in Jamaica in the 1930s on a particular interpretation of the Bible). Marijuana legalization was supported by Rastafarians, and Stephen Marley is an outspoken advocate of the marijuana sector. He founded Kaya Farms, a private marijuana enterprise. Furthermore, he serves on the board of directors of Med-X, a medical marijuana company.
