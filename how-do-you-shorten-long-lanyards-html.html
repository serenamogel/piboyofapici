What is the significance of the term “lanyard”?

The word lanyard is derived from the French word “laniere,” which literally translates as “strap” or “thong.” We’re used to seeing some fairly elaborate lanyards nowadays, but historically, basic straps made of rope or cord found aboard the ship and wound around a pistol, sword, or whistle were the norm for a long time.
