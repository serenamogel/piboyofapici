Sweetwater Actor Everett Osborne Wiki | Bio

Everett Osborne (age: 29, born on January 1, 1994) is a versatile talent known for his successful stint as a professional basketball player in Australia and his recent foray into acting. At 29, he has already made his mark in both fields.

Osborne’s acting abilities recently caught the eye of the audience and critics alike, thanks to his lead role as Nat Clifton in the film “Sweetwater.” The movie marked an impressive debut for him, and he received rave reviews for his performance.

Despite the apparent differences between acting and sports, Everett has found many similarities. Both require hard work, discipline, and training to excel. Additionally, “Sweetwater” proved to be the ideal platform for him to showcase his abilities in both fields.

Everett Osborne Early Life, Parents, and Education

Everett Osborne was born on January 1, 1994, in Pacific Palisades, a neighborhood in Los Angeles, California. His parents played a significant role in his upbringing and supported him in pursuing his passions for acting and sports.

From a young age, Osborne showed an interest in performing and began learning how to read scripts and act on stage at Faithful Resurrection Baptist Church when he was just four years old. Later on, he continued to develop his acting skills at Ivanna Chubbuck’s Acting Studio, a world-renowned institution known for producing talented actors.

In addition to acting, Osborne also had a love for basketball. He played professionally in Australia for over three seasons, where he also pursued his acting career. Osborne’s passion for both sports and acting came together on a global platform when he landed the principal role of “Donte Grand” in the award-winning short film “Nike: Want It All.”

Everett Osborne Acting Journey 

Everett Osborne’s acting journey has been hard work, dedication, and passion. He has starred in various projects and has proved his mettle as a versatile actor.

His career took off after he graduated from the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley with a Bachelor’s degree in Psychology. He landed a role in Scorpion Productions’ “One Hour/Bobby” voice-over, which paved the way for other significant voice-over roles, including the NCAA’s “If You Can Play” campaign.

In 2021, Osborne starred in the music video for Ty Dolla $ign song “By Yourself.” He also appeared in several commercials, including Powerade’s “Power In Numbers” and Chase Bank’s “Younger Self,” directed by Dave Meyers and Pat Hall, respectively.

His dedication and hard work paid off when he got the lead role in the film “Sweetwater,” in which he played Nat ‘Sweetwater’ Clifton. This biopic portrays the life of the first African-American to sign an NBA contract. Osborne’s professional basketball experience in Australia served him well in this role.

In addition to his impressive film credits, Everett acted on television. He guest-starred in BET’s “Sistas” and played the lead guest role in NBC’s “Chicago Fire” season 10, directed by Brenna Malloy.
